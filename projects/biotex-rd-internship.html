Biotex R&D Mechanical Engineering Internship

Hands-on mechanical design, prototyping, test fixture, and documentation experience in a regulated medical device R&D environment.

Overview

At Biotex, I supported non-proprietary R&D mechanical engineering work related to medical device manufacturing and testing support. My work involved CAD/design updates, prototype iteration, fixture concepts, safety improvements, aluminum enclosure concepts, catheter guidance ideas, and engineering documentation.

Problem Context

R&D teams supporting medical device manufacturing and testing need practical fixtures, clean enclosure concepts, repeatable test setups, and documentation that can stand up in a regulated engineering environment.

My Role

  • Supported mechanical design, prototyping, testing, and manufacturing fixture concepts for R&D and manufacturing support workflows.
  • Worked with SolidWorks/CAD, design updates, manufacturing considerations, fixture layouts, and documentation.
  • Contributed to concepts involving automated needle handling, UV curing chamber refinement, catheter guidance fixtures, safety improvements, and aluminum enclosure design.
  • Gained exposure to cGMP and ISO 13485-style engineering documentation practices.

Design / Engineering Approach

  • Kept early concepts prototype-friendly so mechanical assumptions could be tested quickly.
  • Considered manufacturability, assembly access, operator use, safety, and documentation needs together.
  • Used CAD iteration to communicate concepts and evaluate fixture/enclosure architecture.

Selected Technical Decisions

  • Prototype speed vs. mechanical fidelity: used quick concepts to expose fit and workflow issues before more detailed design effort.
  • Fixture repeatability vs. operator access: considered how retention, guidance, and loading features would affect both consistency and usability.
  • Panel/enclosure refinement vs. manufacturability: balanced safety and clean packaging with practical aluminum fabrication and assembly.
  • Documentation effort vs. design maturity: captured enough information to support regulated engineering review without overstating early concepts.

What This Demonstrates to Employers

This experience demonstrates hands-on R&D mechanical design, fixture concept development, regulated documentation awareness, and practical prototype iteration in a medical-device environment.

Outcome / What I Learned

The internship helped me understand how mechanical design decisions connect to testing support, regulated documentation, manufacturing constraints, and practical R&D execution. It also strengthened my interest in medical device R&D, catheter systems, surgical robotics, and mechanism-heavy design roles.
