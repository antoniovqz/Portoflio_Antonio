RF Balloon Catheter / HLHS Research

Fabrication and evaluation of RF balloon catheter prototype concepts for fetal hypoplastic left heart syndrome research.

Catheter prototypingBenchtop testingTissue interactionDesign iteration

Overview

This project involved fabricating and evaluating catheter prototype concepts in a research context related to fetal hypoplastic left heart syndrome. The work emphasized careful iteration, tissue interaction assessment, and benchtop/tissue-based testing.

Problem

Catheter concepts for delicate cardiovascular applications require attention to geometry, guidance, stability, tissue interaction, and repeatable evaluation methods.

My Role

I supported prototype fabrication and evaluation, including guide/frame iteration, benchtop testing, tissue-based testing, and assessment of how prototype features interacted with target tissue.

Design / Engineering Approach

  • Fabricated and adjusted prototype features to improve usability and evaluation consistency.
  • Used benchtop and tissue-based observations to guide iteration.
  • Focused on tissue interaction, prototype handling, and mechanical stability during evaluation.

Key Technical Decisions

  • Prioritized controlled evaluation setups that could reveal mechanical and tissue interaction issues early.
  • Iterated guide/frame details based on physical test observations.
  • Balanced prototype simplicity with enough fidelity to evaluate key research questions.

Outcome / What I Learned

The work built my understanding of catheter prototyping, cardiovascular device constraints, and the importance of benchtop testing before more advanced validation.
