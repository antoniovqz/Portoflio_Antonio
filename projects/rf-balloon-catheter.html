RF Balloon Catheter / HLHS Research

Fabrication and evaluation of RF balloon catheter prototype concepts for fetal hypoplastic left heart syndrome research.

Catheter prototypingBenchtop testingTissue interactionDesign iteration

Overview

This project involved fabricating and evaluating RF balloon catheter prototype concepts in a research context related to fetal hypoplastic left heart syndrome. The work emphasized careful iteration, tissue interaction assessment, and benchtop/tissue-based testing.

Problem

Catheter concepts for delicate cardiovascular applications require attention to geometry, guidance, stability, tissue interaction, and repeatable evaluation methods. Small changes in frame or guide behavior can affect placement, contact, and test repeatability.

My Role

I supported prototype fabrication and evaluation, including guide/frame iteration, benchtop testing, tissue-based testing, and assessment of how prototype features interacted with target tissue.

Design / Engineering Approach

  • Fabricated and adjusted catheter prototype features to improve handling and evaluation consistency.
  • Iterated guide/frame details to improve alignment, stability, and interaction with test tissue.
  • Used benchtop and tissue-based observations to identify where geometry, flexibility, or positioning needed refinement.
  • Focused on tissue interaction, prototype handling, and mechanical stability during evaluation.

Selected Technical Decisions

  • Frame stiffness vs. conformability: stiffer features can improve guidance, while compliant features may reduce unwanted tissue interaction.
  • Prototype fidelity vs. iteration speed: early builds needed enough realism to test behavior without slowing learning cycles.
  • Bench testing vs. tissue-based testing: bench setups improved repeatability, while tissue testing revealed interaction details that simple fixtures could miss.
  • Guide geometry vs. catheter handling: guide features had to support placement without making the prototype difficult to manipulate.

What This Demonstrates to Employers

This project demonstrates catheter-system prototyping, benchtop testing, design iteration, and cardiovascular device research experience.

Outcome / What I Learned

The work built my understanding of catheter prototyping, cardiovascular device constraints, tissue interaction, and the importance of benchtop testing before more advanced validation.
