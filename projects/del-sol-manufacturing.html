Del Sol Foods Manufacturing Internship

Manufacturing process improvement work involving production data, SOPs, quality documentation, equipment evaluation, and expansion planning.

Manufacturing engineeringProcess improvementSOPsEquipment evaluation

Overview

This internship strengthened my practical manufacturing engineering foundation through process improvement, documentation, production data, cross-functional equipment evaluation, and expansion planning.

Problem

Production expansion and process improvement required evaluating equipment options, reducing labor burden, documenting procedures, supporting quality-focused manufacturing work, and balancing capital cost with operator workflow.

My Role

I supported production data review, SOP development, quality documentation, vendor/equipment evaluation, and bakery expansion planning.

Design / Engineering Approach

  • Reviewed process needs and equipment options against operational constraints.
  • Helped document procedures and quality-related information for repeatable production.
  • Worked cross-functionally to connect equipment decisions with labor and cost impacts.

Selected Technical Decisions

  • Automation/equipment investment vs. labor savings: compared equipment cost against expected labor reduction and process reliability.
  • New equipment vs. existing workflow fit: evaluated whether additions would improve throughput without creating operator bottlenecks.
  • Documentation detail vs. usability: SOPs needed enough detail for consistency while remaining practical for production teams.
  • Capital savings vs. performance needs: identified lower-cost options while preserving required process capability.

What This Demonstrates to Employers

This project demonstrates manufacturing engineering, process improvement, documentation discipline, and practical cost/labor tradeoff analysis.

Outcome / What I Learned

Helped evaluate/procure equipment for a bakery expansion project, reducing labor requirements by about 30% and identifying approximately $2,000 in capital expenditure savings. The work sharpened my manufacturing engineering, documentation, and cross-functional communication skills.
