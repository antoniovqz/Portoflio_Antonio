ANGEL Soft Robotic Gripper

Soft robotic harvesting gripper research involving TPU prototypes, geometry optimization, cable routing, motor spool design, controls, and experimental testing.

Soft roboticsTPU 3D printingDynamixel motorsExperimental testing

Overview

I co-authored publication work on a soft robotic harvesting gripper intended to improve grasp stability while reducing damaging contact stress through compliant TPU structures.

Problem

Soft robotic grippers for harvesting need to securely grasp irregular objects while limiting contact stress, distributing force, and maintaining stable motion through flexible mechanisms and cable-driven actuation.

My Role

I supported CAD, TPU prototype design and 3D printing, geometry optimization, cable routing, motor spool geometry, Dynamixel motor integration, current-based PD control, publication contribution, and experimental testing.

Design / Engineering Approach

  • Iterated TPU gripper geometry to reduce contact stress and improve grip stability.
  • Worked through cable routing and motor spool details that affected actuation consistency, tension path, and repeatability.
  • Integrated Dynamixel motor behavior and current-based PD control considerations with the mechanical prototype.
  • Connected mechanical prototype behavior with experimental grip observations and force-distribution goals.

Selected Technical Decisions

  • TPU compliance vs. stiffness: softer structures improved conformability, while stiffness was needed for repeatable force transfer.
  • Cable routing path vs. friction: cleaner routing reduced losses, but geometry had to fit within the printed gripper body.
  • Motor spool diameter vs. resolution/force: spool geometry affected cable travel, torque demand, and control sensitivity.
  • Grip stability vs. contact stress: finger geometry had to hold the target without concentrating force in damaging regions.

What This Demonstrates to Employers

This project demonstrates robotics hardware, soft actuator/mechanism design, experimental testing, and mechatronics integration.

Outcome / What I Learned

This project strengthened my skills in soft robotics, mechatronics, CAD-to-prototype iteration, experimental testing, and the interaction between mechanical design and control behavior.
