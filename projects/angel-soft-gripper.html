ANGEL Soft Robotic Gripper

Soft robotic harvesting gripper research involving TPU prototypes, geometry optimization, cable routing, motor spool design, controls, and experimental testing.

Soft roboticsTPU 3D printingDynamixel motorsExperimental testing

Overview

I co-authored publication work on a soft robotic harvesting gripper intended to improve grasp stability while reducing damaging contact stress.

Problem

Soft robotic grippers for harvesting need to securely grasp irregular objects while limiting contact stress and maintaining stable motion through compliant structures.

My Role

I supported CAD, TPU prototype design and 3D printing, geometry optimization, cable routing, motor spool geometry, Dynamixel motor integration, current-based PD control, and experimental testing.

Design / Engineering Approach

  • Iterated TPU gripper geometry to reduce contact stress and improve grip stability.
  • Worked through cable routing and motor spool details that affected actuation quality.
  • Connected mechanical prototype behavior with control and testing observations.

Key Technical Decisions

  • Used compliant material behavior as a design variable rather than treating flexibility as a side effect.
  • Balanced grip stability with contact stress reduction.
  • Evaluated performance experimentally to guide geometry and actuation decisions.

Outcome / What I Learned

This project strengthened my skills in soft robotics, mechatronics, CAD-to-prototype iteration, experimental testing, and the interaction between mechanical design and control behavior.
