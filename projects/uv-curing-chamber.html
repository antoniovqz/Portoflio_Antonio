UV Curing Chamber Redesign

Mechanical redesign of enclosure and fixture concepts to improve user safety, catheter guidance, mounting, light-leak reduction, and manufacturability.

Enclosure designDFMAluminum panelsSafety-conscious design

Overview

This project explored a refined UV curing chamber concept while preserving assumed electronics and UV source constraints. The work centered on a panelized aluminum enclosure, fixture base integration, catheter routing, fastener planning, and user-safety improvements.

Problem

The existing setup needed improved user safety, better catheter guidance, cleaner fixture mounting, stronger enclosure refinement, and reduced risk of UV light leakage during use.

My Role

I contributed mechanical enclosure and fixture concepts, including aluminum panel layouts, M3 tapped-hole planning, front-panel organization, catheter pass-through/gland ideas, gasket/hinge concepts, and manufacturability-oriented design updates.

Design / Engineering Approach

  • Developed panelized aluminum concepts with tapped M3 fastener layouts, removable panels, and assembly access in mind.
  • Organized front-panel features around user access, part loading, cable/catheter routing, and clean mechanical interfaces.
  • Explored hinge, gasket, and overlap concepts to improve usability while reducing light leakage paths.
  • Considered catheter pass-through/gland concepts and fixture-base integration for repeatable positioning.
  • Balanced enclosure refinement with practical machining, sheet/plate fabrication, and assembly constraints.

Selected Technical Decisions

  • Panelized enclosure vs. one-piece housing: panelization improved access, serviceability, and fabrication practicality.
  • M3 tapped-hole layout vs. through-bolts: tapped panels reduced exposed hardware and simplified assembly where material thickness allowed it.
  • Gasketed/overlapped seams vs. simple butt joints: light containment improved, but geometry had to remain manufacturable.
  • Dedicated catheter gland vs. open slot: a gland concept better controlled routing and light leakage, while an open slot would be easier to fabricate.

What This Demonstrates to Employers

This project demonstrates enclosure design, safety-conscious mechanical design, DFM, fixture integration, and regulated hardware thinking.

Outcome / What I Learned

The redesign strengthened my enclosure design, fixture integration, and DFM instincts. It also reinforced how safety, assembly sequence, access, and manufacturability need to be addressed early in R&D hardware concepts.
