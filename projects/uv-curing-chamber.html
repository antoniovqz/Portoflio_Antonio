UV Curing Chamber Redesign

Mechanical redesign of enclosure and fixture concepts to improve user safety, catheter guidance, mounting, light-leak reduction, and manufacturability.

Enclosure designDFMAluminum panelsSafety-conscious design

Overview

This project explored a refined UV curing chamber concept while preserving assumed electronics and UV source constraints. The work centered on enclosure layout, fixture integration, fastener planning, catheter routing, and user-safety improvements.

Problem

The existing setup needed improved user safety, better catheter guidance, cleaner fixture mounting, stronger enclosure refinement, and reduced risk of light leakage during use.

My Role

I contributed mechanical enclosure and fixture concepts, including aluminum panel layouts, front-panel organization, catheter pass-through ideas, and manufacturability-oriented design updates.

Design / Engineering Approach

  • Developed aluminum panel concepts with tapped M3 fastener layouts and assembly access in mind.
  • Explored hinge, gasket, and front-panel ideas to improve usability and light containment.
  • Considered catheter pass-through/gland concepts and fixture-base integration for repeatable positioning.
  • Balanced enclosure refinement with practical manufacturing and assembly constraints.

Key Technical Decisions

  • Used a panelized enclosure approach to simplify fabrication and maintenance access.
  • Focused on user-facing safety features and reduced light leakage without overcomplicating the mechanism.
  • Kept fixture integration modular so test components could be updated independently.

Outcome / What I Learned

The redesign strengthened my enclosure design, fixture integration, and DFM instincts. It also reinforced how safety, assembly sequence, access, and manufacturability need to be addressed early in R&D hardware concepts.
