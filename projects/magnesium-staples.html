Magnesium Surgical Staple Testing

Mechanical testing and failure characterization of post-bent bioabsorbable magnesium surgical staple samples.

Mechanical testingBiomaterialsStress-strain analysisFailure characterization

Overview

This research project focused on evaluating post-bent bioabsorbable magnesium surgical staples through mechanical testing, degradation/corrosion evaluation, and analysis of mechanical behavior.

Problem

Bioabsorbable surgical staple concepts must balance geometry, forming effects, mechanical strength, degradation behavior, and failure modes. Post-bending behavior is especially important because bend geometry can influence yield and fracture characteristics.

My Role

I contributed to tensile stress-strain testing and analysis of elastic/inelastic behavior, yield behavior, failure points, bend geometries, and degradation/corrosion testing.

Design / Engineering Approach

  • Used tensile testing to evaluate mechanical response after bending.
  • Reviewed stress-strain behavior to identify elastic region, inelastic deformation, yield behavior, and failure points.
  • Connected bend geometry and degradation context to observed mechanical performance.

Key Technical Decisions

  • Focused analysis on mechanical behavior that would matter for surgical device function and reliability.
  • Compared sample behavior through consistent test framing rather than isolated single-point observations.
  • Documented failure patterns to support design and material interpretation.

Outcome / What I Learned

The project strengthened my foundation in biomaterials, test planning, stress-strain interpretation, and failure characterization for medical device research.
