Magnesium Surgical Staple Testing

Mechanical testing and failure characterization of post-bent bioabsorbable magnesium surgical staple samples.

Mechanical testingBiomaterialsStress-strain analysisFailure characterization

Overview

This research project focused on evaluating post-bent bioabsorbable magnesium surgical staples through tensile testing, degradation/corrosion evaluation, and analysis of mechanical behavior after forming.

Problem

Bioabsorbable surgical staple concepts must balance geometry, forming effects, mechanical strength, degradation behavior, and failure modes. Post-bending behavior is especially important because bend radius and local strain can influence yield, fracture, and corrosion-sensitive failure behavior.

My Role

I contributed to tensile stress-strain testing and analysis of elastic vs. inelastic behavior, yield behavior, failure points, bend geometries, and degradation/corrosion testing context.

Design / Engineering Approach

  • Used tensile testing to evaluate mechanical response after staple bending and forming.
  • Reviewed stress-strain behavior to identify elastic region, onset of inelastic deformation, yield behavior, and failure points.
  • Compared bend geometry effects to understand how local deformation influenced mechanical performance.
  • Connected degradation/corrosion context to observed strength and failure behavior.

Selected Technical Decisions

  • Elastic vs. inelastic behavior: separated recoverable deformation from permanent deformation to interpret forming effects.
  • Yield point vs. ultimate failure: tracked both because surgical device reliability depends on deformation onset as well as fracture.
  • Bend geometry comparison: treated bend features as design variables that could change local stress concentration and failure mode.
  • Degradation context vs. dry mechanical strength: considered corrosion/degradation effects alongside baseline mechanical performance.

What This Demonstrates to Employers

This project demonstrates medical-device testing, biomaterials research, failure analysis, and data-driven engineering judgment.

Outcome / What I Learned

The project strengthened my foundation in biomaterials, test planning, stress-strain interpretation, and failure characterization for medical device research.
